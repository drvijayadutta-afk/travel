Frequently asked

What is Panchakarma and how long does it take?

Panchakarma is a classical Ayurvedic detoxification and rejuvenation programme involving five therapeutic procedures to eliminate toxins and restore balance. An authentic Panchakarma requires a minimum of 14 to 21 days under a qualified BAMS doctor. Shorter programmes are wellness breaks, not clinical detox. Kerala is the premier destination, with monsoon season (June to August) considered the optimal treatment window.

What are the best Ayurveda retreats in Kerala?

The best Ayurveda retreats in Kerala include Somatheeram Ayurvedic Resort in Kovalam (35 national awards, international guest experience), Arya Vaidya Sala Kottakkal (150-year legacy, NABH accredited), Nila Ayurveda in Wayanad (boutique, doctor-supervised), and Sreerudra Ayurveda Hospital (NABH, specialising in spinal and joint conditions). The right centre depends on your health goals, duration, and budget.

Can Indians travel to New Zealand for wellness retreats?

Yes. New Zealand has a growing number of wellness retreat centres offering yoga, meditation, nature immersion, and holistic healing. Indian nationals require a New Zealand visitor visa. We assist with visa documentation guidance and manage full trip coordination including flights, accommodation, and retreat booking.

What is the difference between a wellness retreat and a spa holiday?

A spa holiday is primarily relaxation-focused with treatments as add-ons to a holiday. A wellness retreat is structured around health transformation: a medical or clinical foundation (Ayurveda, naturopathy), daily discipline (diet, yoga, rest), and a specific health goal (detox, weight management, stress recovery, chronic disease management). The outcomes are measurably different, and the commitment required is also different.
