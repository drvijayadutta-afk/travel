Frequently asked

What is medical tourism in India?

Medical tourism in India involves patients travelling — domestically or internationally — to access high-quality, affordable healthcare at NABH or JCI-accredited hospitals. India's medical tourism market was valued at USD 11.14 billion in 2025 and is growing at 12.4% CAGR. Common treatments include cardiac surgery, joint replacement, oncology, fertility treatment, dental care, and cosmetic procedures — at 40-80% lower cost than the USA, UK, or Australia.

What are the best wellness retreats in India?

The best wellness retreats in India include Ayurveda and Panchakarma centres in Kerala, yoga and meditation retreats in Rishikesh, naturopathy centres in Coimbatore, luxury wellness resorts like Ananda in the Himalayas and Vana Retreat in Uttarakhand, and boutique wellness properties in Coorg and Wayanad. The right choice depends on your health goals, duration, and budget.

How do I apply for an e-Medical Visa for India?

The e-Medical Visa is available to citizens of 167 countries and can be applied for online at the Indian Visa Online portal. You will need your hospital appointment confirmation, a passport-size photo, and your passport details. The visa is typically processed within 1-4 business days. An e-Medical Attendant Visa is also available for one accompanying family member. We manage the full visa application process for all our international patients.

How much does medical treatment in India cost compared to other countries?

Medical treatment in India is typically 40-80% less expensive than equivalent procedures in the USA, UK, or Australia. For example, cardiac bypass surgery costs approximately USD 5,000-7,000 in India versus USD 70,000-150,000 in the USA. Hip replacement in India costs USD 6,000-8,000 versus USD 40,000-50,000 in the USA. These savings more than offset travel and accommodation costs, even for international patients.

What is the difference between wellness tourism and medical tourism?

Medical tourism involves travelling to access specific clinical treatments — surgery, diagnostics, fertility treatment, or specialist consultations — at accredited hospitals. Wellness tourism involves travelling for preventive, restorative, or holistic health experiences — Ayurveda, yoga, naturopathy, spa therapy, or mindfulness retreats — that enhance wellbeing rather than treat a diagnosed condition. Many travellers combine both: medical treatment followed by a wellness recovery retreat.
